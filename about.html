The Dummer Legacy

In the spring of 1950, master miller Henry Dummer converted his grain storage barn into a small milling operation with a singular vision: to produce the finest flour using traditional methods. What began as a modest enterprise serving three local bakeries has grown into one of New England's most respected flour mills, while remaining true to Henry's original commitment to quality.

Today, under the leadership of Henry's grandson Richard, we continue to honor our heritage while innovating to meet the needs of modern bakers and chefs. Our mill still stands on its original foundation, a testament to the enduring value of craftsmanship.

Seven Decades of Excellence

Our journey from a local mill to regional leader

1950

Founding of Dummer Flour Mills

Henry Dummer converts his grain barn into a milling operation with a single stone mill, serving local bakeries in Coos County, New Hampshire.

1968

First Expansion

Added second stone mill and began supplying restaurants throughout New Hampshire and Vermont. Henry's son William joins the business.

1985

Modernization Era

Installed state-of-the-art milling equipment while maintaining traditional quality standards. Began supplying specialty food stores throughout New England.

2002

Organic Certification

Became one of the first mills in the region to offer certified organic stone-ground flours. Richard Dummer becomes CEO.

Present

Regional Leader

Now supplying premium flours to over 200 bakeries, restaurants, and retailers across New England while still operating from our original location with the same commitment to quality.
